The loadout

Scroll below and grab your essential kit to live off the land

Remember - 1lb on your feet is 5lbs on your back. Don't overpack!

A ruck size of around 35 litres will allow you to carry the minimum and ensures you will not carry anything unnecessary.

 

Knife

Arguably the most indispensible survival tool.

A knife can be used for foraging, food preparation, self-defence, fire starting, shelter building and general utility.

Ensure you choose a full-tang knife, where the blade is one piece, with the handle attached to it. A partial tang blade is susceptible to breaking under duress.

My recommendation:

SOG Seal Pup

Canteen

A canteen is invaluable as a means of keeping drinking water at hand at all times.

The typical military style canteen has a 1 litre capacity. The average person, when active outdoors, will require around 2 litres per day.

The mouth of a canteen is wide enough to drop water purification tablets into. The high-density polyethylene contruction is practically indestructable and will easily withstand falls.

My recommendation:

Nalgene 1 litre canteen

Multitool

Packing a heavy punch for it's compact size, a multitool condenses a variety of hand tools into a small package.

With the other modern items in the survival kit, you will be certain to need a multitool for changing batteries, cutting wire, or opening cans.

Many variants exist such as credit card sized, or balisong-style mechanisms for including pliers.

My recommendation:

Leatherman OHT 16-in-One

Compass

The compass has been a staple navigation tool for every outdoorsman for centuries. Although heavily modernised, the core idea stays the same.

A compass allows the user to find their bearing on a map, visualise waypoints, and give a better sense of direction.

Choosing one with an illuminated dial will aid you in navigating in low-light situations.

My recommendation:

Silva Ranger S

Flashlight

Although the melatonin in our eyes can help our vision adapt surprisingly well to the darkness, a source of illumination is essential for avoiding hazards and signalling.

For navigating or creating shelter in low-light situations, a headlamp with allow you to do so hands-free.

Choosing a light with a red filter will preserve the concentrations of melatonin in your eyes, sparing your natural night vision.

My recommendation:

Petzl Taktikka

Lighter

Because you can't light a cigar with a flint striker. A lighter is a welcome tool when it comes to quickly starting a fire with little effort and high reliability.

In desperate circumstances, the fuel from the lighter can be used as a fire source.

Ideally, multiple fire starting tools should be carried. The weight penalty is negligible in relation to the importance of their use.

My recommendation:

Zippo, Chrome finish

Paracord

Another essential multi-use item, cordage has as many applications as your imagination can stretch.

Some common sense uses would be securing shelter, crafting a fishing line, or thread for sewing. A little more left-field could be dental floss, an emergency tourniquet , or for saving your friends from quicksand

While tree vines or regular twine can do the job, parachute cord is a favourite for it's durability and ability to be dissected into smaller strands.

My recommendation:

550 Paracord

Ballistic Helmet

For survivalists who hate having spare cash in their pocket. A ballistic helmet can withstand gunshot threats up to .44 magnum

In rock climbing or urban situations, concussions are a reality that can be avoided with a brain bucket.

Modern special forces have adopted high-cut ballistic helmets allowing communications devices to rest on the users ears unobstructed.

My recommendation:

Ops-Core FAST Ballistic

First Aid Kit

A simple infection can turn fatal if untreated. The probability of such injuries are a lot higher when navigating outdoors away from emergency services.

A good place to start of is with a store bought kit as these will contain the essentials such as crepe bandages and antibiotics.

An individual first aid kit is best created by the user to fit their requirements. While some may be bought off the shelf, they are not a "one size fits all". Consider the user's unique needs, such as asthma inhalers or other medications.

My recommendation:

Custom made by user

Gas Mask

House fires, volcano ash, mustard gas, nuclear fallout.

A respirator allows you to breathe in dire circumstances where clean air is unobtainable.

Modern respirators have a ballistic panoramic visor to avoid tunnel vision associated with classic claustrophobic gas masks.

My recommendation:

MSA Millenium

Mobile Phone

Worth it's weight in gold, a cheap cellular device will put you in contact with the outside world and get your ass home.

The famous (original!) Nokia 3310 will stand the test of time. With 55-hours talk time you can be sure it won't run out of charge faster than you can say Steve Jobs.

Joking aside, it can be used for timekeeping, providing entertainment with Snake, and clubbing predators to death.

My recommendation:

Nokia 3310
