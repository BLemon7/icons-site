The loadout

Scroll below and grab your essential kit to live off the land

Remember - 1lb on your feet is 5lbs on your back. Don't overpack!

A ruck size of around 35 litres will allow you to carry the minimum and ensures you will not carry anything unnecessary.

 

Knife

Arguably the most indispensible survival tool.

A knife can be used for foraging, food preparation, self-defence, fire starting, shelter building and general utility.

Ensure you choose a full-tang knife, where the blade is one piece, with the handle attached to it. A partial tang blade is susceptible to breaking under duress.

My recommendation:

SOG Seal Pup

Canteen

A canteen is invaluable as a means of keeping drinking water at hand at all times.

The typical military style canteen has a 1 litre capacity. The average person, when active outdoors, will require around 2 litres per day.

The mouth of a canteen is wide enough to drop water purification tablets into. The high-density polyethylene contruction is practically indestructable and will easily withstand falls.

My recommendation:

Nalgene 1 litre canteen

Compass

The compass has been a staple navigation tool for every outdoorsman for centuries. Although heavily modernised, the core idea stays the same.

A compass allows the user to find their bearing on a map, visualise waypoints, and give a better sense of direction.

Choosing one with an illuminated dial will aid you in navigating in low-light situations.

My recommendation:

Silva Ranger S

Flashlight

Althogh the melatonin in our eyes can help our vision adapt surprisingly well to the darkness, a source of illumination is essential for avoiding hazards and signalling.

For navigating or creating shelter in low-light situations, a headlamp with allow you to do so hands-free.

Choosing a light with a red filter will preserve the concentrations of melatonin in your eyes, sparing your natural night vision.

My recommendation:

Petzl Taktikka

Lighter

Becuase you can't light a cigar with a flint striker. A lighter is a welcome tool when it comes to quickly starting a fire with little effort and high reliability.

In desperate circumstances, the fuel from the lighter can be used as a fire source.

Ideally, multiple fire starting tools should be carried. The weight penalty is negligible in relation to the importance of their use.

My recommendation:

Zippo, Chrome finish

Mobile Phone

Worth it's weight in gold, a cheap cellular device will put you in contact with the outside world and get your ass home.

The famous (original!) Nokia 3310 will stand the test of time. With 55-hours talk time you can be sure it won't run out of charge faster than you can say Steve Jobs.

Joking aside, it can be used for timekeeping, providing entertainment with Snake, and clubbing predators to death.

My recommendation:
